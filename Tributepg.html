Captain Vikram Batra

Captain Vikram Batra was an Indian Army officer. He was posthumously awarded the Param Vir Chakra, the highest Indian military decoration, for his actions during the Kargil War.

About the Legend

Early Life:
Vikram Batra was born on 9 September 1974 in a small town in Palampur, Himachal Pradesh to a Punjabi Hindu family.

Education:
Completed his schooling from D.A.V. Public School, Palampur.
Graduated from Government College, Palampur.

Military Career:
Joined the Indian Military Academy (IMA) in 1996.
Commissioned into the Indian Army as a Lieutenant in 1997.
Served in the Jammu and Kashmir Rifles regiment.

Operation Vijay (Kargil War):
During the Kargil War in 1999, Captain Vikram Batra played a crucial role.
Known for his bravery and leadership, he led his troops in difficult and high-altitude warfare conditions.

Key Achievements and Actions:
Led the capture of Point 5140, a strategic peak at a high altitude in the Kargil region, on June 20, 1999, under heavy enemy fire.
His famous war cry during the capture was "Yeh dil maange more!" ("This heart wants more!").

Martyrdom:
On July 7, 1999, during the operation to recapture Point 4875, he was fatally wounded while rescuing an injured officer.
Awarded the Param Vir Chakra posthumously, India's highest military award for bravery in the face of the enemy.

Legacy:
Captain Vikram Batra's bravery and sacrifice are remembered as exemplary.
His life and actions have inspired many Indians, and he remains a symbol of courage and patriotism.

Honors and Tributes:
Apart from the Param Vir Chakra, various institutions and memorials have been named after him to honor his memory and sacrifice.
Bollywood movie "Shershaah" (2021) is based on his life, bringing his story to a wider audience.

Personal Life:
Known for his cheerful personality and strong leadership qualities, he was admired by his peers and subordinates alike.
